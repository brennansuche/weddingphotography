Best Wedding Venues
in British Columbia

By Brennan Suche  —  Wedding Photographer, Available Nationally

There is no province in Canada with a greater range of wedding backdrops than British Columbia. Within a few hours of Vancouver, you can find yourself in front of a glacier, on the edge of a Pacific cliff, beneath old-growth forest, or surrounded by vines in the Okanagan valley. The raw material for remarkable wedding photography is extraordinary here.

I travel to BC for weddings regularly. These are the venues I'd recommend without hesitation — and the places that have produced some of the most visually striking work I've done.

"BC's landscapes are so inherently dramatic that almost any venue in the right setting becomes a backdrop that does half the work for you. The challenge is finding places where the human side of the wedding — the flow, the intimacy, the logistics — is as carefully considered as the view."

Vancouver & The Lower Mainland

Brock House Restaurant

Perched directly on the water in Point Grey, Brock House has been one of Vancouver's most beloved wedding venues for decades. The oceanfront location, the mature trees, and the heritage architecture create ceremony and portrait settings that are difficult to replicate elsewhere in the city. The light over English Bay in the late afternoon is extraordinary — warm, directional, and consistent in a way that makes photographs look effortless. For couples who want a Vancouver venue that genuinely connects them to the Pacific, this is the starting point.

The Wedding Garden at Northlands

A private estate in North Vancouver set against the backdrop of the mountains, with gardens, mature trees, and a ceremony space that frames the North Shore peaks behind your guests. The combination of curated garden setting and dramatic mountain backdrop is unique in the Lower Mainland, and the venue operates with enough care that the logistics are reliable.

UBC Botanical Garden

For intimate ceremonies, UBC's Botanical Garden offers something genuinely rare in an urban environment — a sense of being surrounded by living, growing things, with spaces that feel far removed from the city despite being minutes from downtown. The photography potential in the garden's varied microclimates and textures is remarkable, and the setting suits couples who value nature and understated beauty over grandeur.

Seasons in the Park

Set in Queen Elizabeth Park with panoramic views of Vancouver and the North Shore mountains, Seasons is one of the most scenically positioned venues in the city. The 360-degree views mean there's almost always a beautiful direction to photograph regardless of where the light is coming from, and the park's quarry gardens provide portrait locations that are quietly spectacular.

Whistler & The Sea-to-Sky

Nita Lake Lodge

Whistler's most intimate luxury option, Nita Lake Lodge sits on the edge of a glacially fed lake with mountains rising on every side. It's a smaller property than some of the resort options in the village, which works in its favour — the scale is human, the service is attentive, and the lakeside ceremony location is one of the most serene and beautiful settings I've photographed anywhere in Canada. The reflections on the lake at sunrise are extraordinary.

Fairmont Chateau Whistler

When scale and spectacle are the priority, the Chateau delivers. The mountain backdrop, the grand architecture, and the resort's capacity to handle large destination weddings with full accommodation make it a logical choice for couples with significant guest lists. The photography during summer and fall — when the surrounding mountains are at their most dramatic — is consistently stunning.

Squamish Lil'wat Cultural Centre

A striking architectural venue in Whistler that honours the cultures of the Squamish and Lil'wat peoples. The building itself is remarkable — cedar and stone, with a quality of design and craft that makes it inherently photogenic. The surrounding forest and mountain setting give the venue a depth of place that more generic event spaces simply can't replicate.

The Okanagan

Summerhill Pyramid Winery

One of Canada's most visually distinctive wineries, Summerhill has a ceremony amphitheatre with lake views, organic vineyard grounds, and a setting that feels genuinely original. The Okanagan light in late summer and fall — dry, warm, and long — is remarkable for photography, and the combination of vineyard landscape and lake views provides a variety of portrait settings within a single property.

Burrowing Owl Estate Winery

In Oliver, near the southern end of the Okanagan Valley, Burrowing Owl is a serious winery estate with grounds, accommodation, and a ceremony terrace that looks out over vineyards toward the valley. It's a destination venue that suits couples willing to build a weekend around the wedding rather than just an evening — the property rewards that kind of investment with scenery and service that make the trip feel like an occasion.

CedarCreek Estate Winery

Kelowna's CedarCreek is one of the Okanagan's most polished winery venues — beautifully landscaped grounds, a new event pavilion with lake views, and the kind of mature vineyard setting that photographs beautifully in any season. The combination of the winery's aesthetic care and the inherent drama of the Okanagan landscape makes it among the most reliably spectacular venues in BC.

Vancouver Island & The Gulf Islands

The Wickaninnish Inn

On the wild Pacific coast near Tofino, the Wickaninnish sits directly above Chesterman Beach with the open ocean on the horizon. There is no more dramatic wedding setting in Canada. The inn itself is beautifully designed — cedar and glass, warm and considered — and the beach ceremonies it hosts are unlike anything available in the rest of the country. For couples who want a destination wedding that genuinely earns the word "destination," this is the standard.

Brentwood Bay Resort

On the Saanich Inlet north of Victoria, Brentwood Bay offers waterfront ceremony and reception spaces with a level of intimacy and natural beauty that the larger Victoria venues can't match. The inlet setting, the old-growth Douglas firs, and the resort's thoughtful design make it a strong choice for smaller destination weddings where the quality of experience matters more than capacity.

Hastings House Country Estate

On Salt Spring Island, Hastings House is a Relais & Chateaux property with heritage buildings, formal gardens, and farm grounds on a working estate. The combination of English country house aesthetics and West Coast landscape is unique in Canada, and the island setting means weddings here have a genuine remoteness and sense of occasion that mainland venues struggle to match. The ferry crossing to Salt Spring is itself part of the experience.

Travelling to British Columbia

I travel to BC regularly for weddings and am always glad to do it. The province offers photographic opportunities that are genuinely unmatched in Canada, and the couples who choose BC venues tend to be people who care about place — which makes for remarkable photographs.
