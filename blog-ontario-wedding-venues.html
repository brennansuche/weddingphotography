Best Wedding Venues
Across Ontario

By Brennan Suche  —  Wedding Photographer, Available Nationally

Ontario is one of the most varied wedding destinations in the country. Within a few hours of Toronto, you can find yourself at a vineyard overlooking Lake Ontario, deep in the forest at a private estate, on the edge of a lake in cottage country, or in a heritage barn that's been standing for over a century.

I photograph weddings across the province — from the Toronto core to Prince Edward County, the Muskokas, Niagara, and beyond. These are the Ontario venues I return to most, and the ones I'd point a couple toward without hesitation.

"Ontario's best wedding venues share something in common: they feel like they belong somewhere. Not like a generic event space, but like a place with a history and a character that becomes part of your day."

Niagara & Wine Country

Ravine Vineyard Estate Winery

Set in St. Davids in the heart of Niagara wine country, Ravine is a working vineyard with a farmhouse aesthetic and genuinely beautiful grounds. The ceremony lawn overlooks vines, the light in the late afternoon is warm and directional, and the heritage barn and farmhouse interiors give indoor coverage real character. Couples who want something that feels rooted in the land rather than built for events will find it here.

Pillitteri Estates Winery

One of Niagara's most elegant vineyard venues. The estate has a classic quality that works beautifully for formal weddings, and the grounds offer multiple ceremony and portrait locations across a single property. The surrounding vineyards make for remarkable sunset portraits, and the scale of the estate handles larger guest lists without feeling impersonal.

Balls Falls Conservation Area

A heritage property that most couples outside the region don't know about, which is exactly why I mention it. The historic mills and falls at Balls Falls create ceremony and portrait settings unlike anything else in Ontario — water, stone, and old-growth trees with a quality of light that photographs beautifully at almost any time of day.

Prince Edward County

Huff Estates Winery

The County has become one of Ontario's most sought-after wedding destinations, and Huff Estates is among the reasons why. The winery sits on gently rolling land with Lake Ontario visible in the distance, and the property has a warmth and informality that suits couples who want a relaxed, wine-country celebration. The light in early evening here is remarkable — long, golden, and soft in a way that makes portraits essentially effortless.

The Waring House

A historic inn and restaurant in Picton that has been hosting weddings for decades. The grounds include gardens, an orchard, and a restored heritage building with genuine character. It suits intimate weddings particularly well — the scale of the property means a smaller guest list feels intentional and considered rather than modest.

Sandbanks Provincial Park

For couples who want something genuinely different, Sandbanks offers elopements and small ceremonies on Lake Ontario's freshwater dunes — one of the most visually striking natural settings in the province. The photography potential here is extraordinary. It takes planning and permitting, but for couples willing to put in the work, the results are unlike anything else available in Ontario.

Muskoka & Cottage Country

Windermere House Resort

A landmark Muskoka resort that has been hosting celebrations on Lake Rosseau for over 150 years. The white clapboard exterior, the water views, and the boathouse provide an authentically Muskoka backdrop — the kind that makes the setting feel earned rather than manufactured. Summer weddings here are consistently beautiful, and the resort handles logistics competently enough that couples can focus on the day itself.

Taboo Muskoka Resort

A more contemporary Muskoka option, Taboo has striking architecture and lakefront grounds that work well for couples who want the Muskoka setting without the historic-resort aesthetic. The property is large, the light over the lake in the evening is excellent, and the resort can accommodate destination-style weddings with accommodation for guests on-site.

Deerhurst Resort

One of the most complete wedding properties in cottage country. Deerhurst has been a resort destination on Peninsula Lake for over a century, and its facilities and experience show. The waterfall ceremony location on the property is one of my personal favourites in Muskoka — a genuinely spectacular setting with moving water, mature trees, and a sense of place that photographs beautifully.

Heritage & Rural Ontario

Kurtz Orchards

In Niagara-on-the-Lake, Kurtz Orchards is a working apple orchard and event property with a relaxed, pastoral quality that suits couples looking for something warm and unpretentious. The ceremony barn, the orchard rows, and the surrounding farmland provide a variety of portrait settings within a single property, and the late-season weddings here — when the trees are heavy with fruit — are genuinely beautiful.

Bellamere Winery and Event Centre

Located just outside London, Ontario, Bellamere is a full-service estate venue with vineyard grounds and a well-designed event space. It's a strong option for couples in southwestern Ontario who want the vineyard aesthetic without travelling to Niagara, and the property handles larger weddings comfortably.

Paletta Lakefront Mansion

Burlington's Paletta Mansion sits directly on the Lake Ontario waterfront and offers ceremony and reception spaces with unobstructed water views. The heritage architecture, the grounds, and the lakefront setting make this one of the more photogenic venues in the greater Toronto area — close enough to the city to be accessible, far enough to feel like a destination.

A Note on Travelling

I'm based in Toronto but I photograph weddings across Ontario and nationally. If you're planning a wedding outside the city — in the County, in Muskoka, in Niagara, or further afield — I'd be glad to come to you. Travel is part of the work, and some of the most memorable weddings I've photographed have been in places that required a drive.

If you want to talk through venues from a photography perspective — what I've shot, what I'd recommend, what questions to ask — reach out. That conversation is free and genuinely useful.
