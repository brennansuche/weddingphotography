The Best Wedding Venues
in Toronto

By Brennan Suche  —  Wedding Photographer

"The best venue for your wedding is the one that fits how you actually want the day to feel — not just the one that photographs well. But when those two things align, something special happens."

Historic & Grand

Casa Loma

If you want a wedding that feels genuinely cinematic, Casa Loma delivers. The castle — yes, there is a castle in Toronto — sits on a hill above the city with sweeping skyline views, stone corridors, and gardens that were built for portrait photography. The light in the conservatory in the afternoon is extraordinary. It's a popular venue, which means you'll need to plan around their event calendar, but for couples who want scale and grandeur, nothing in the city quite matches it.

The Carlu

One of Toronto's most beautiful Art Deco spaces. The Round Room at The Carlu has a domed ceiling, rich colour, and proportions that make even a moderately sized guest list feel like an event. The venue is in the heart of downtown, which makes logistics easy, and the detail work throughout the space gives photographers an enormous amount to work with during cocktail hour and reception coverage.

Eglinton Grand

Elegant without being stiff. The Eglinton Grand has the kind of bones that make a wedding feel considered — high ceilings, warm tones, and a ballroom that works for both intimate gatherings and larger celebrations. The bridal suite is one of the better ones in the city from a photography standpoint, with natural light that holds well into the afternoon.

Intimate & Architectural

Malaparte (TIFF Bell Lightbox)

For couples who care about design, Malaparte is a genuinely exciting venue. The rooftop terrace looks out over the Entertainment District and King West, and the space itself has a clean, modern quality that pairs well with both formal and casual weddings. Sunsets from this terrace are consistently dramatic, and the interior has a gallery-like quality that gives receptions a sense of occasion without excess.

The Burroughes

A beautifully restored historic building in the heart of Queen West. The Burroughes has exposed brick, large windows, and a warm industrial quality that photographs exceptionally well. It suits couples who want a venue with character but without the formality of a ballroom — the kind of place where you can see yourself actually having dinner with the people you love, rather than performing a wedding at them.

Steam Whistle Brewing

Unexpected, but consistently impressive. The Roundhouse building has extraordinary heritage architecture — vaulted ceilings, industrial columns, and natural light that filters through large windows in a way that's genuinely cinematic. It's a non-traditional choice, but for couples who want something original, it's one of the most photographically interesting spaces in the city.

Garden & Outdoor

Edwards Gardens

One of the city's most underrated ceremony venues. The gardens are immaculate, and in late spring and early summer the floral density makes every background effortlessly beautiful. Edwards Gardens works particularly well for smaller ceremonies where the intimacy of the garden setting feels intentional rather than like a budget compromise.

Graydon Hall Manor

A short drive from the downtown core, Graydon Hall is a manor house set in landscaped grounds that make it feel far more rural than it actually is. The ceremony space in the courtyard is one of my favourite spots in the broader Toronto area — the architecture frames guests naturally and the light in the late afternoon is consistently beautiful. It's a full-service venue, which simplifies planning considerably.

The Doctor's House

Just north of the city in Kleinburg, The Doctor's House is a heritage property that sits in a village setting unlike anything else in the Toronto area. The grounds are expansive, the interior spaces are warm and characterful, and the surrounding village makes for remarkable portrait locations. For couples willing to travel slightly, it's one of the most complete venues in the region.

What to Look for
As a Couple

Beyond aesthetics, there are a few things I always encourage couples to think about when evaluating Toronto venues. First: natural light. How does the ceremony space handle afternoon light? Are there windows? What direction do they face? This matters more than most couples realize until they see their photos.

Second: flow. A venue that makes logical sense — where cocktail hour, ceremony, and reception spaces connect naturally — leads to a day that feels easier for everyone, including your photographer. Venues where guests are constantly moving between floors or buildings break momentum in ways that are hard to recover from.

Third: ask about exclusivity. Some of Toronto's most beautiful venues share their spaces between multiple events on the same day. That's not necessarily a dealbreaker, but it's worth knowing upfront.
